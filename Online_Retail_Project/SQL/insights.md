# 📊 Online Retail Data Analysis – Key Insights

## 💰 Overall Business Performance

* Total **delivered revenue** is approximately **£5.77M**, generated from **13,891 orders**.
* The dataset includes **3,248 unique customers** (excluding null values).
* The overall **order cancellation rate is low (~1.83%)**, indicating efficient order fulfillment and stable operations.

---

## 📦 Product Performance Insights

* **MEDIUM CERAMIC TOP STORAGE JAR** is the top product by gross quantity (76,296 units), but has extremely high cancellations (74,477 units), reducing its net sales significantly.
* Products such as:

  * **WORLD WAR 2 GLIDERS ASSTD DESIGNS**
  * **JUMBO BAG RED RETROSPOT**
    show strong performance in both gross and net sales, indicating **consistent demand and low cancellation impact**.
* Several products rank high in gross sales but drop in net rankings, suggesting:

  * Potential product issues
  * Customer dissatisfaction
  * Operational inefficiencies

---

## 💸 Revenue-Based Product Insights

* Top revenue-generating products include:

  * **REGENCY CAKESTAND 3 TIER**
  * **PARTY BUNTING**
* Non-product entries like:

  * **POSTAGE**
  * **DOTCOM POSTAGE**
  * **Manual adjustments**
    contribute significantly to total revenue, indicating the presence of **additional transactional or operational charges**.
* Some products disappear in net revenue rankings, highlighting the **impact of cancellations on actual earnings**.

---

## 👥 Customer Behavior Insights

* Revenue is highly concentrated among a **small group of high-value customers**, with the top customer contributing over **£148K**.
* There is a clear distinction between:

  * **High-value customers** → fewer orders but large spending 💰
  * **Frequent customers** → many orders but lower spending per order 🛒
* Minimal overlap between top revenue and top order customers suggests **diverse customer segments**.

---

## 📈 Sales & Growth Trends

* Revenue appears significantly higher in 2011 compared to 2010; however, this is influenced by **unequal data coverage**:

  * 2010 → only December
  * 2011 → January to August
* Within 2011:

  * **May and June** are peak revenue months
  * **August** shows a sharp decline (likely due to incomplete data)

---

## 📅 Seasonality & Monthly Trends

* Revenue remains relatively strong from **January to July**, with a peak around mid-year.
* December (2010) shows high revenue despite limited data, indicating **strong holiday season demand 🎄**.
* Monthly order trends closely follow revenue patterns, confirming:

  > Higher order volume directly drives higher revenue.

---

## 🛒 Customer Spending Behavior (AOV)

* Overall **Average Order Value (AOV) ≈ £500**, indicating moderate spending per transaction.
* Monthly AOV trends show:

  * Highest in **August (~£555)** → fewer but higher-value orders
  * Lowest in **April (~£431)** → more price-sensitive purchases

👉 This suggests varying customer purchasing behavior across different periods.

---

## ⚠️ Data Limitations

* Some cancellation records do not have corresponding original transactions, indicating **dataset incompleteness**.
* Therefore:

  * **Delivered (gross) metrics are used as the primary basis for analysis**
  * Net metrics are considered **indicative rather than absolute**

---

## 🧾 Final Summary

The analysis reveals strong overall business performance with low cancellation rates and consistent product demand. However, it also highlights:

* Significant variation in product reliability
* Diverse customer behavior patterns
* Seasonal trends in revenue and orders

A small group of customers contributes disproportionately to revenue, while certain products experience high cancellation rates, indicating opportunities for **targeted business improvements and optimization strategies**.
